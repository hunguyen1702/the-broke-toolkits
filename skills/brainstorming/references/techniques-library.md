# Brainstorming Techniques Library

62 techniques across 9 categories. Browse by category or search by name.

## Collaborative (5 techniques)

- **Yes And Building** — Build momentum through positive additions. Each idea becomes a launching pad using prompts like "Yes, and we could also..." to create energetic collaborative flow.
- **Brain Writing Round Robin** — Silent idea generation followed by building on others' written concepts. Gives quieter voices equal contribution through writing, passing, and building cycles.
- **Random Stimulation** — Use random words or images as creative catalysts to force unexpected connections. Breaks through mental blocks with serendipitous inspiration.
- **Role Playing** — Generate solutions from multiple stakeholder perspectives to build empathy while ensuring comprehensive consideration. Embody different roles and ask what matters most to them.
- **Ideation Relay Race** — Rapid-fire idea building under time pressure. Structure with 30-second additions and fast passing to maintain creative momentum and prevent overthinking.

## Creative (11 techniques)

- **What If Scenarios** — Explore radical possibilities by questioning all constraints. "What if we had unlimited resources?" "What if the opposite were true?" Perfect for breaking through stuck thinking.
- **Analogical Thinking** — Find solutions by drawing parallels to other domains. Transfer successful patterns by asking "This is like what?" and "What other examples come to mind?"
- **Reversal Inversion** — Deliberately flip problems upside down to reveal hidden assumptions. "What if we did the opposite?" Great when conventional approaches fail.
- **First Principles Thinking** — Strip away assumptions to rebuild from fundamental truths. "What do we know for certain?" Essential for breakthrough innovation.
- **Forced Relationships** — Connect unrelated concepts to spark innovative bridges. Take two unrelated things, find connections, and explore how they could work together.
- **Time Shifting** — Explore solutions across different time periods. "How would this work in the past?" "What about 100 years from now?" Reveals constraints and opportunities.
- **Metaphor Mapping** — Use extended metaphors as thinking tools. Transforms abstract challenges into tangible narratives by mapping metaphor elements to problem elements.
- **Cross-Pollination** — Transfer solutions from completely different industries or domains. "How would industry X solve this?" Sparks breakthrough innovations.
- **Concept Blending** — Merge two or more existing concepts to create entirely new categories. Goes beyond simple combination to genuine innovation.
- **Reverse Brainstorming** — Generate problems instead of solutions. "How could we make this fail?" Reveals hidden opportunities and unexpected pathways.
- **Sensory Exploration** — Engage all five senses to discover multi-dimensional solution spaces. What does the idea feel, smell, taste, or sound like?

## Deep (10 techniques)

- **Five Whys** — Drill down through layers of causation to uncover root causes. Ask "Why did this happen?" repeatedly until reaching fundamental drivers.
- **Morphological Analysis** — Systematically explore all possible parameter combinations. Identify key parameters, list options for each, try combinations, and spot patterns.
- **Provocation Technique** — Use deliberately provocative statements to extract useful ideas from absurd starting points. "What if [provocative statement]?" Then extract the useful principle.
- **Assumption Reversal** — Challenge and flip core assumptions to rebuild from new foundations. "What assumptions are we making?" Essential for paradigm shifts.
- **Question Storming** — Generate questions before seeking answers. Only questions, no answers yet. Ensures you're solving the right problem.
- **Constraint Mapping** — Identify and visualize all constraints. Which are real vs. imagined? Find pathways around or through limitations.
- **Failure Analysis** — Study successful failures to extract valuable insights. What went wrong, why, and how to apply that wisdom now?
- **Emergent Thinking** — Allow solutions to emerge organically without forcing linear progression. What patterns emerge? What wants to happen naturally?
- **Observer Effect** — Recognize how observing, measuring, or defining solutions changes their behavior. Ask: "How does our framing shape what we see? What would we notice if we looked differently?"
- **Hidden Connections** — Explore how seemingly unrelated solution elements might be deeply linked. What hidden dependencies or synergies exist between components that we're not seeing?

## Introspective Delight (6 techniques)

- **Inner Child Conference** — Channel pure childhood curiosity and wonder. Ask what 7-year-old you would ask. Use "why why why" questioning. Forbid boring thinking.
- **Shadow Work Mining** — Explore what you're actively avoiding or resisting. Where's the resistance? What scares you? Mine the shadows for buried wisdom.
- **Values Archaeology** — Excavate deep personal values driving decisions. What really matters? Why do you care? What's non-negotiable?
- **Future Self Interview** — Seek wisdom from your wiser future self. What would 80-year-old you say? Gain long-term perspective through temporal self-mentoring.
- **Body Wisdom Dialogue** — Let physical sensations and gut feelings guide ideation. What does your body say? Where do you feel it? Trust tension and physical cues.
- **Permission Giving** — Grant explicit permission to think impossible thoughts. Break self-imposed creative barriers. Explore, try, experiment freely.

## Structured (7 techniques)

- **SCAMPER Method** — Systematic creativity through seven lenses: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse. Methodical product improvement and innovation.
- **Six Thinking Hats** — Explore problems through six distinct perspectives: White (facts), Red (emotions), Yellow (benefits), Black (risks), Green (creativity), Blue (process).
- **Mind Mapping** — Visually branch ideas from a central concept. Put main idea in center, branch concepts, identify sub-branches. Perfect for organizing complex thoughts.
- **Resource Constraints** — Generate solutions by imposing extreme limitations. What if you had only $1? No technology? One hour? Forces essential priorities and creative efficiency.
- **Decision Tree Mapping** — Map all possible decision paths and outcomes. Visualize complex choice architectures to reveal hidden opportunities and risks.
- **Solution Matrix** — Create a grid of problem variables and solution approaches. Test combinations to find optimal pairings and discover gaps.
- **Trait Transfer** — Borrow attributes from successful solutions in unrelated domains. What traits make success X work? How to transfer those traits here?

## Theatrical (6 techniques)

- **Time Travel Talk Show** — Interview past, present, and future selves for temporal wisdom. A playful method for gaining perspective across different life stages.
- **Alien Anthropologist** — Examine familiar problems through completely foreign eyes. Become an alien observer. What seems strange? Reveals hidden assumptions.
- **Dream Fusion Laboratory** — Start with impossible fantasy solutions, then reverse-engineer practical steps. Makes ambitious thinking actionable through backwards design.
- **Emotion Orchestra** — Let different emotions lead separate brainstorming rounds, then harmonize. Explore angry, joyful, fearful, and hopeful perspectives.
- **Parallel Universe Cafe** — Explore solutions under alternative reality rules. Different physics, alternative social norms, changed historical events.
- **Persona Journey** — Embody different archetypes or personas. How would the persona solve this? What does the character see that normal thinking misses?

## Wild (8 techniques)

- **Chaos Engineering** — Deliberately break things to discover robust solutions. What if everything went wrong? Build anti-fragility by stress-testing ideas.
- **Guerrilla Gardening Ideas** — Plant unexpected solutions in unlikely places. Where's the least expected place? Grow solutions underground with stealth innovation.
- **Pirate Code Brainstorm** — Take what works from anywhere and remix without permission. Encourages rule-bending rapid prototyping and maverick thinking.
- **Zombie Apocalypse Planning** — Design solutions for extreme survival scenarios. Strip away all but essential functions to find core value.
- **Drunk History Retelling** — Explain complex ideas with uninhibited simplicity. No filter, raw thoughts. Removes overthinking barriers to find raw truth.
- **Anti-Solution** — Generate ways to make the problem worse. How to sabotage this? What would make it fail spectacularly? Reveals hidden assumptions.
- **Quantum Superposition** — Hold multiple contradictory solutions simultaneously until the best emerges through observation and testing.
- **Elemental Forces** — Imagine solutions sculpted by natural elements. How would earth, fire, water, or air shape this? Tap into primal creative energies.

## Biomimetic (3 techniques)

- **Nature's Solutions** — Study how nature solves similar problems. What ecosystems provide parallels? Adapt biological strategies from 3.8 billion years of evolution.
- **Ecosystem Thinking** — Analyze the problem as an ecosystem. Identify symbiotic relationships, natural succession, and ecological principles for systems thinking.
- **Evolutionary Pressure** — Apply evolutionary principles: selective pressure, adaptation, survival of the fittest. How would evolution optimize this?

## Cultural (4 techniques)

- **Indigenous Wisdom** — Draw upon traditional knowledge systems and indigenous approaches. What ancestral wisdom guides us? Access overlooked problem-solving methods.
- **Fusion Cuisine** — Mix cultural approaches like fusion cuisine. What happens when mixing culture A with B? What cultural hybrids emerge?
- **Ritual Innovation** — Apply ritual design principles to create transformative experiences. What ritual would transform this? Use anthropological insights.
- **Mythic Frameworks** — Use myths and archetypal stories as frameworks. What myth parallels this? How does mythic structure inform the solution?
